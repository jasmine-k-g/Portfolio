Seismic Test
Sound and words by Jasmine Griffiths
Voice by Sophie Harvey

Seismic Testing is a method of mapping the sea floor by shooting air guns toward the bottom of the ocean, then recording the sound that reflects back up with an array of hydrophones, and analysing this data. The sound levels used in seismic testing are very high, up to 250dB. This can be disruptive and harmful to sea life.

This sound work was made in response to the proposed seismic testing by CGG (an oil and gas company, a.k.a. Veridian) in the Otway Basin off the coasts of Victoria, in waters frequented by endangered Pygmy Blue Whales and in proximity to the endangered Southern Right Whale's calving ground - the only calving ground for Southern Right Whale's eastern population. Whales are particularly at risk of being affected by seismic testing, because their range of hearing and communication overlaps heavily with the frequencies of the airgun blasts.
